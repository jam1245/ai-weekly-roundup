# LM Organizational Context Reference

**Purpose:** Grounding material for RMS Relevance sections and the Strategic Picture. Use this to name specific internal initiatives, use correct LM terminology, and write for the actual audience — not a generic defense contractor.

---

## The Audience

**Who they are:** 500+ DoD program management practitioners across the AI Community of Practice (US-EPT-AI-Solutions-CoP), primarily within RMS. The newsletter lands across all four BAs, but RMS is home.

**Their day job:** Program Managers and PM-adjacent roles — schedule planners, finance leads (F&BO), supply chain leads, engineering leads — who own program outcomes in a matrix organization. They live inside EVM compliance, IMS maintenance, risk reviews, and customer deliverables. They know when a relevance connection is real versus generic.

**What lands:**
- Specific numbers, attributed correctly — pass rates, investment figures, headcount, revenue
- Stories that map to something they're actively dealing with (schedule compression, reporting burden, risk identification, workforce load)
- Connections to internal initiatives they recognize by name
- Peer comparisons — what are other primes or defense contractors doing?

**What falls flat:**
- "AI is transforming industries" — not a relevance statement
- Bullet summaries instead of narrative
- Vague connections ("could help with program management broadly")
- Hype without grounding metrics

## Internal Initiatives & Platforms — Use These by Name

### AI Factory
LM's scalable, secure, open-architecture AI ecosystem. This is the primary internal anchor for any story touching AI infrastructure, model deployment, enterprise platform strategy, or vendor lock-in risk. When a story is about how companies are building internal AI capability vs. buying from hyperscalers — or about platform consolidation — AI Factory is the RMS Relevance hook.

### EP&T AI Program
Enterprise Performance & Transformation (EP&T) sponsors the AI CoP and this newsletter. EP&T's mandate: enable exceptional business performance by assessing risk, driving transformation, training PM and planning professionals, and fostering continuous improvement. EP&T is described internally as a "trusted catalyst" for financial, customer, and shareholder commitments. When a story touches PM tooling, AI-assisted planning, or workforce productivity, EP&T is the internal anchor.

### AI Community of Practice (US-EPT-AI-Solutions-CoP)
The 500+ practitioner community this newsletter serves. Spans RMS primarily, draws from all four BAs. The footer on every edition. The CoP exists to share AI best practices relevant to program management execution.

### OneLM
LM's unified cultural framework spanning all four BAs. Three core behaviors: Collaborate to Win, Accelerate Change, Hear and Be Heard. Relevant when a story touches enterprise-wide AI rollout, change management, or the cultural and psychological dimensions of AI adoption at scale.

### Program as a Unit of Action (PUA)
Core LM cultural concept — all employees take collective ownership for program outcomes regardless of function. The framing for cross-functional AI integration stories. When a story is about breaking down silos through shared AI tooling, PUA is the relevance frame.

## Full Spectrum Leadership Imperatives

LM's six leadership behaviors — relevant when a story touches AI strategy, organizational decision-making, or culture:

- **Deliver Results** — consistently meet expectations through innovative solutions and partnerships
- **Sound Decisions** — timely, sound business decisions in the best interest of LM and stakeholders
- **Shape the Future** — envision the future state, articulate the path, foster a culture of innovation
- **Build Relationships** — strong relationships within LM and with customers/stakeholders
- **Energize the Team** — positive, inclusive environment where people develop and excel
- **Model Excellence** — ethical behavior, ownership at all levels

*"Shape the Future" and "Sound Decisions" are the most natural hooks when stories touch AI strategy and decision-making risk.*

## What NOT to Write in RMS Relevance

Even with this context loaded, avoid:

- "This could help LM stay competitive" — too vague
- "AI adoption is accelerating across industries" — that's the story, not the relevance
- "LM should consider implementing..." — the roundup informs, it doesn't prescribe
- Referencing internal initiatives the audience doesn't recognize — if you name something, it must be real
- Invented LM executive quotes or positions not sourced from the article
